What is Postpartum Depression?

PPD is a complex mix of physical, emotional, and behavioral changes that occur in some women after giving birth. According to the DSM-5, a manual used to diagnose mental disorders, PPD is a form of major depression that begins within 4 weeks after delivery.

Postpartum Depression Treatment

Postpartum depression is treated differently depending on the severity of the symptoms. Anti-anxiety or antidepressant medications, psychotherapy, and participation in a support group for emotional support and education are all treatment options.

Types of Postpartum Depression

Three terms are used to describe the mood changes that women can experience after giving birth:

    1. Baby Blues
    2. Postpartum Depression (PPD)
    3. Postpartum Psychosis
